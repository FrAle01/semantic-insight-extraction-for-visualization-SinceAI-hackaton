PART 2: CHART RECOMMENDATIONS

**1. Chart Title:**  
Average Processing Time by Observation Type

- **Chart Type:** Bar Chart
- **Visual Mapping (The Blueprint):**
    - X-Axis: observation_type
    - Y-Axis: avg_processing_time_days (Average)
    - Color/Segment: observation_type (distinct color per type, with "traffic light" logic: red for above-average, green for below-average)
- **Reasoning:**  
A bar chart allows for clear comparison of average processing times across all observation types. Using color to highlight types with above-average processing times (e.g., red) immediately draws attention to potential problem areas, aligning with HSE best practices for risk visualization.

**2. Chart Title:**  
Proportion of Observations with Above-Average Processing Time by Type

- **Chart Type:** Donut Chart (if <5 types) or 100% Stacked Bar (if more)
- **Visual Mapping (The Blueprint):**
    - Segments: observation_type
    - Value: Count of observation_type where avg_processing_time_days > overall average
    - Color/Segment: observation_type (with "traffic light" logic)
- **Reasoning:**  
This chart shows the share of each observation type that exceeds the overall average processing time, making it easy to spot which types are most frequently delayed. This supports decision-making by focusing on outliers.

**3. Chart Title:**  
Distribution of Processing Times Across Observation Types

- **Chart Type:** Box Plot
- **Visual Mapping (The Blueprint):**
    - X-Axis: observation_type
    - Y-Axis: avg_processing_time_days
    - Color/Segment: observation_type
- **Reasoning:**  
A box plot (if sufficient data points per type) would reveal the spread and outliers in processing times for each observation type, helping to identify not just averages but also variability and extreme cases.
